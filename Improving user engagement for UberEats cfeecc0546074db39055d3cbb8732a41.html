Background information

Uber Eats is a three-sided online food delivery platform launched by Uber in 2014. The app connects users with a broad range of local restaurants and food, so users can order from the full menus whenever they want. Apart from ordering, people can also view menus of the restaurant, track food order and leave reviews or ratings for the restaurant.

Defining business objective

Business model

UberEats acts as an intermediary among different participants on its platform, facilitating the process of food ordering between partnered restaurant and customers. As an intermediary, UberEats earns revenues from the following source:

Commission fee per successful order: For every order successfully purchased on its platform, UberEats will take a commission fee of its total cost. Partnered restaurants are often responsible to pay this fee.

Delivery fees: For every order picked up and delivered to customers, UberEats takes a flat fee while the rest goes to driver. UberEats requires a flat fee irrespective of the size of the order. Users are often responsible to pay this fee

Brand advertising: UberEats earns money from collaborating with giant brands such as McDonald, Burger King. These corporate partners will pay UberEats for providing advertisings, promotion campaign or exclusive deals.

Company mission and strategy

Uber's mission: "We ignite opportunity by setting the world in motion". The company seems to position itself as a technology platform that's changing any type of user needs that involve "moving"

Uber's strategy: To understand how UberEats fits in overall strategy of Uber, I looked at Uber's strategy overall and come to the following conclusion regarding its strategic moves:

  • Prioritize user growth through extensive outreach to urban and suburban area
  • Invest in technology to create exceptional user experience, guaranteeing to deliver food for customers in less than 30 minutes
  • Minimize operation and acquisition cost through the synergy with Uber's main ride haling business

Proposing solutions

Pain point 1: My order gets delayed

I have browsed through quite a number of user complaints on App store and realized that there are two major causes of delayed order.

The first reason is due to lack of drivers at the time orders are made, which usually happens when customers order past midnights or just late evenings when restaurants are about to close down.

The second reason is customers's address are hard for drivers to find. Most of UberEats drivers in the US deliver food by cars. For customers who live in apartments or in areas that require drivers to park, it can cause some trouble to navigate. In suburban area especially, if customers live in areas that are not car-friendly, it will take drivers longer time to figure out shortcuts.

  • Proposed solutions

    Solution 1: If customers order food in the evening, check if it's has passed the restaurant's closing time. If customers order food within a 15 minutes timeframe before the restaurant is about to close, send an alert to customers to make them aware about potential extra delay. By giving customers the power to make a decision when they are fully aware of involved risks, customers will feel less frustrated even if the delay happens.

    Solution 2: Customers can tolerate some mistakes if they receive on-time explanation on their food delay instead of letting them feel clueless about what's going on. When an order takes longer than initial estimation to reach customers, show a notification in the app telling customers why it is delayed. This can be something such as "We are sorry for the inconvenience. The restaurant is running a little slow today due to high demand".

    Solution 3: Increase the number of available drivers in non car-friendly areas by creating a program that encourages people who have a bike to become UberEats's deliveryman. This can be college students who want to earn extra money, or fitness enthusiasts who want to get pocket money while doing exercise. I think this will also support Uber's value in general considering how much the company is committed to sustainability.

Pain point 4: There is missing items in delivery

Missing items in delivery is rooted in 2 causes: either the restaurant missed the portion by accident or drivers take the food for themselves.

Drivers cheating customers's food is certainly unacceptable. While this issue significantly harms user experience and that drivers should be held accountable, it is also important to understand why drivers do such acts. It can be due to "a moment of sinful temptation" as they are working late at night and get hungry, or they have been too busy to buy a meal, or potentially they want to save money.

On the other hands, it can be due to a bad habit they have, even though there are no real external difficulties.

Another possibility for missing items is that drivers only have a summary of the order. They don't have full visibility on what should be included in the order so that they will take whatever said to be ready by restaurant.

The following solutions are proposed to resolve issues from both restaurants's side and drivers's side.

  • Proposed solutions

    Solution 4: Require restaurants to take photo of the food before it is shipped and update the photo on UberEats's platform. Users should be able to view photos as their order delivery status changes to 'On the way'. If missing item is then delivered to customers, customers should have the option to notify UberEats on its platform easily.

    Solution 5: Allow users to make a checklist on the oder's details before drivers come and pick it up

    Solution 6: Before users confirm an order, allow them to select whether they want their drivers to check the food before it is shipped or not. Users can pay a small fee if they select this option (0.5c - $1). This would give drivers more incentive to make sure the order is completed whole

    Solution 7: Offer drivers who take late night shift different incentives to encourage them to successfully order a delivery. The incentives can range from special tips from UberEats for "late night snack" or a free meal for every 10 successful orders shipped in late night.
