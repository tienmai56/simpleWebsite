Background information

Market Place is a feature on facebook application that allows people to buy, sell and discover things around them. It was launched in 2016, branding itself as a 'social marketplace' since there's a social touch on this e-commerce feature. Sellers and buyers take to each other through messenger, and both parties can check each other's facebook profile as the transaction takes place totally on facebook.

Business objective

I chose this business objective for 2 reasons:

  • Support Facebook's business model: Facebook's main revenue stream comes from ads. Advertisers set up marketing campaign tailored to the target demographic, behavior, background, and many other attributes. Social features on Facebook, including Market Place, exist to retain users longer on its platform. User engagement is therefore extremely important because high engagement rate can translate into ad views.
  • Company's mission: Facebook's mission statement is “to give people the power to share and make the world more open and connected". While marketplace certainly supports this mission of Facebook by connecting retailers to customers, we can only truly evaluate its efficiency by looking at how users are engaging with each other.

I will focus on buyers group for now because:

  • Buyer groups make up for a much higher volume of users on Facebook marketplace than sellers. Their engagement is crucial as they indirectly bring in revenues for Facebook by viewing ads created by advertisers on the platform
  • Buyers are very important for sellers when considering whether sellers would join Facebook marketplace or not. Facebook itself is not a typical e-commerce platform such as Amazon or E-bay. A lot of services such as shipping, return or customer review are not offered. The best competitive advantage that Facebook marketplace can offer to sellers is its vast network of users, who can potentially turn to buyers. Maintaining a high user engagement for buyers therefore has a direct impact on the growth of sellers

User pain points & Prioritization

Before diving into user pain points, it's important to remember what goals buyer group is trying to achieve.

Primary goal: to buy something that fits their need and budget
Secondary goal: to discover good stuff around them but not necessarily going to purchase

With these goals in mind, I list down key pain points faced by buyer group below. But before that, this is a simple framework I'm using to prioritize User pain points:

  • High : Has a significant impact on user journey by generating 1 or more barriers that prevents them from achieving primary goal
  • Medium: Reduces user experience in overall, but a temporary solution is already provided
  • Low - Poses minor nuisance to user experience

Proposed improvements

Pain point 1: I get bombarded by irrelevant "today picks" products that I don't have any interest in

  • Proposal 1: Understand their interest and needs first before offering them product picks. For new users, when they go on marketplace for the first time we can offer a one-time pop-up that requires users to select some interest (i.e cars, sports, etc). Users will see top product pick based on their selection each time they go on market place.
  • Proposal 2: Allow returning users to customize their own "today picks" based on price preference, location, number of inquiries, date listed, flash sale, etc
  • Proposal 3: Group items under "product pick" into one single cart, or let user scroll horizontally instead of vertically, so that users don't feel overwhelmed by many choices.

Pain point 6: I cannot find what I am looking for

  • Proposal 4 : Allow users to set up notification when a product matching their needs is listed
  • Proposal 5: Let users subscribe to relevant buy & sell group that may offer what they are looking for in the future

Based on the analysis above, I will focus on the following improvement proposals :

  1. Must have:
    • Create a one-time pop-up that requires new marketplace users to select some interest (i.e cars, sports, etc) at their arrival
    • Allow returning users to customize their own "today picks" based on price preference, location, number of inquiries, date listed, flash sale, etc
  1. Good to have:
    • Allow users to set up notification when a product matching their needs is listed
  1. Nice to have:
    • Group items under "product pick" into one single cart, or let user scroll horizontally instead of vertically, so that users don't feel overwhelmed by many choices. (can do A/B testing for this feature improvement)

Metrics to measure success

I will be looking at the following metrics BEFORE versus AFTER the improvements are implemented:

  • Click through rate in product pick: are users clicking through top product pick more after it is tailored to their personal preference?
  • Average time spent on product pick page : are users spending more time browsing item on product pick page?
