Background information

Duolingo is the most popular language-learning platform and the most downloaded education app in the world, with more than 300 million users. The company's mission is to make education free, fun, and accessible to all. Duolingo is designed to feel like a game and scientifically proven to be effective. In addition to its core platform, the company created the Duolingo English Test, an affordable and convenient language certification option that is accepted by over 2000 universities. The company uses the freemium model; the app and the website are accessible without charge, although Duolingo also offers premium services for a fee.

Business objective

I chose this business objective for 2 reasons:

  1. Based on the company mission: Duolingo brands itself as trying to make high-quality education accessible for everyone. With that being said, user engagement is a very important metrics for the company since it reflects how many people find the language curriculum well designed and valuable.
  1. Based on the company business model: Duolingo offers a premium subscription for users with extra content and no ads. Higher user engagements can better help the company in converting users to paid customers

In this particular experiment, I will focus on increasing user engagement for Power users group because:

  1. They deeply care about the curriculum of Duolingo and interact with the app on a daily basis. I think with their investment of time and effort, power users make up for most of the company's "loyal users". Thinking about how to retain their engagement is therefore very important.
  1. They have a much more urgent and serious need to learn a new language than the other groups. With that being said, it's more likely that power users group will spend money on premium description in exchange for more content and no-ads experience.
  1. I also identify myself as a power user of Duolingo so I would be able to provide some hands-on views from my own experience.

Proposed improvements

Pain point 3: Find it difficult users to have a review of what I just learned. There is no place to refer to review all major grammars taught in a certain unit

  • Proposal 1: By the end of each unit, show an option for users to review major grammars that have been taught throughout that unit. This can be done in a form of a hard-coded page or pop-up
  • Proposal 2: Under user account summary page, add a tab that stores all reviews for every unit that user has completed so that it's easier for them to locate whenever they need
  • Proposal 3: by end of each unit, offer a customized popup that tells users what specific knowledge they need to review based on their past mistakes

Pain point 4: Unable to use what I learn to communicate in real life due to lack of speaking practice

  • Proposal 4: design speaking exercises with criteria such as users have to use a minimum number of new words in their response in order to pass. Can use an algorithm to extract user input from the recording and determine the result
  • Proposal 5: design role play exercise between friends. both people participating will be assigned a situation. They can record their response and send the recording inside the chat space as to practice speaking skill together

Metrics to measure success

I will be looking at the following metrics BEFORE versus AFTER the improvements are implemented:

  • Average time per user session : are users spending more time on our platform after the improvements are made?
  • Retention rate : does the retention rate go up after the improvements are made?
  • Conversion rate : does the conversion rate from from free users to paid customers go up after the improvements are made?

Experiment plan

Since these proposals will deeply affect user experience and app design, I would chose greyscale rollout first, meaning we will only roll out the features to a selected market (i.e focus on users on a certain city of a certain country).

If our metrics show negative signs, we will roll back the feature immediately

Go-to-market

  1. Select which market to test this feature rollout first
    1. Make sure this market has a sufficient ratio of power users to give insightful details for further analysis
    1. Make sure this market has financial capacity for bear a tolerable amount of revenue loss, just in case the features do not bring about expected results

  1. Launch campaign
    1. Push notifications to users and offer extra rewards for new feature trials
    1. Send email campaigns to users defined as within power users group
    1. Arrange in-depth user interview to gather feedback for product improvement
